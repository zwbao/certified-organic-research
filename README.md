## The Problem

It's 2026. AI can now:

- 💡 Generate research ideas
- 📚 Do literature reviews without reading a single paper
- 🧪 Design and run experiments
- 📊 Analyze data and produce figures
- 📝 Write the entire manuscript
- 📨 Submit to journals
- 😤 Write rebuttals to Reviewer 2
- 🎉 Probably attend the conference on your behalf next

So what exactly is the researcher doing? **Updating their h-index on Google Scholar.**

We think research should be like food: you deserve to know what's in it.

## The Solution

The **Certified Organic Research License (COR-1.0)** — an open license for research that is genuinely, verifiably, painfully human-made.

## Organic vs. Conventional: A Comparison

| | 🔬 Organic Research | 🏭 Conventional Research |
|---|---|---|
| Hypothesis | Shower thought at 3am | GPT-4 output #47 of 200 |

| Methodology | Trial and error and error and error | AI agent ran 10,000 experiments overnight |

## FAQ

**Q: I used Grammarly. Am I still organic?**
A: Basic Grammarly is fine. Grammarly AI rewriting your entire paragraph? That's a co-author, not a tool.

**Q: I used ChatGPT to understand a concept. Does that count?**
A: Learning is fine. Copy-pasting the explanation into your paper is not. The line is: did the AI help you think, or did it think for you?

**Q: My PI told me to use AI to speed up the paper.**
A: Show them this license. If they insist, add "AI" to the author list. Watch how fast they change their mind.

**Q: What about AI for peer review?**
A: If AI is reviewing AI-written papers, that's just machines talking to machines. At that point, why do we need journals? Just let the AIs email each other.

**Q: How do you verify research is actually organic?**
A: Organic research typically features: unclear figures, inconsistent formatting, at least one paragraph the author clearly wrote at 4am, and a Methods section that says "as previously described" for the hard parts.

**Q: Is this a joke?**
A: Yes. But also, a little bit, no.
